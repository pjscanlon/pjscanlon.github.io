Improving Recruitment Strategies in the National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES)

Recruiting respondents for large-scale federal surveys has become increasingly challenging, and the National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES)—the gold standard in national health examination surveys—is no exception. While its response rates remain comparatively high, the complexity of NHANES’ multi-stage data collection process (household screening, in-home interview, and offsite medical examination) presents unique obstacles to participation. Recognizing the need for a deeper understanding of these challenges, I led a project to gather insights directly from NHANES Field Interviewers (FIs) and Study Managers (SMs). Through a series of structured focus group discussions, we examined barriers to recruitment, management inefficiencies, and opportunities for improvement, leading to concrete recommendations for increasing response efficiency while maintaining data quality.

Goals

  • Investigate declining response rates in NHANES and identify barriers field staff face when recruiting sampled respondents into the study.
  • Assess how program management, survey materials, and communication between headquarters and field teams impact recruitment efficiency.
  • Develop data-driven recommendations to improve response efficiency while maintaining NHANES' role as the gold standard in health examination surveys.

Challenges

  • NHANES' multi-stage data collection makes participation more burdensome than in typical surveys.
  • Field staff face increasing respondent resistance, including anti-government sentiment and refusal patterns that vary by demographics.
  • The decentralized case assignment process led to concerns about inconsistent management, lack of transparency, and communication gaps between headquarters, field offices, and interviewers.

Methods and Tools

  • Conducted and coordinated three focus group discussions with 26 Field Interviewers (FIs) and 3 Study Managers (SMs).
  • Used free-listing techniques to analyze field staff perspectives on job challenges and rewards, identifying the most salient themes.
  • Applied qualitative analysis to pinpoint key barriers to recruitment and management inefficiencies affecting field operations.

Outcomes

  • Communication & Scheduling Improvements: Findings led to recommendations for standardizing case assignments, ensuring all interviewers receive assignments at the same time via a centralized process.
  • Management Consistency: Proposed uniform training, mentoring programs, and quality control procedures to reduce variation across field offices.
  • Survey Incentives & Logistics: Suggested changes to the interview staff incentive structure, reducing competition among interviewers and recognizing contributions across different survey phases.
  • Questionnaire Refinements: Recommended simplifying the NHANES survey instrument to reduce respondent burden and improve data quality.
  • Media & Public Awareness: Identified the need for stronger media outreach before case assignments begin to build trust and increase cooperation rates.
