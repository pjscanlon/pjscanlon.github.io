CDC's Vaccine Hesitancy Questionnaire for Public Health Messaging

Understanding vaccine confidence and hesitancy is critical for shaping effective public health messaging and policy. To support this, I led the development of the CDC’s standard vaccine hesitancy question set, now widely used in national and targeted surveys. This work provides data-driven insights to public health officials, helping them craft optimal messaging strategies to improve vaccine uptake.

Goals

  • Identify how Americans perceive vaccine confidence and hesitancy.
  • Develop a concise, validated question set to measure key factors contributing to hesitancy.
  • Enable nationwide and targeted surveys to inform public health messaging and policymaking.
  • Ensure the question set is methodologically sound and adaptable for use in different survey contexts.

Challenges

  • Capturing the nuanced reasons behind vaccine hesitancy across different populations.
  • Designing questions that are valid, reliable, and unbiased while remaining succinct.
  • Ensuring the question set is broad enough for national use yet granular enough for targeted interventions.
  • Designing a modular question set that can measure both overall vaccine hesitancy and, with simple edits, be adapted to assess hesitancy toward specific vaccines or vaccine-preventable conditions.

Methods

  • Focus Groups: Conducted five focus group discussions across two cities to explore public perceptions of vaccines and the reasons members felt confident and/or hesitant getting vaccines for themselves or their children.
  • Cognitive Interviews: Coordinated and conducted an iterative series of 50 cognitive interviews across five rounds on proposed vaccine hesitance and confidence questions, refining the items for clarity of interpretation and accuracy of response.
  • A/B Testing: Tested different question framings and orderings to optimize measurement accuracy while minimizing response burden.
  • Web Probing: Used embedded cognitive probes in a nationwide web survey to evaluate how well the question set captured vaccine confidence constructs.

Outcomes & Impact

  • Developed the CDC’s standard vaccine hesitancy question set, now used in nationwide and targeted surveys.
  • Findings have directly informed public health messaging strategies, improving the effectiveness of vaccine communication efforts.
  • Research has contributed to multiple published studies analyzing vaccine hesitancy trends in the U.S.
