About me

Growing up I could never latch onto a single area of interest—I was fascinated by botany and history, architecture and politics, food and engineering. But beneath it all, I was fascinated by the stories behind the data we see in books, articles, and advertisements: why did a person answer a question the way they did, what were they thinking when they used an app, how did they navigate a message to uncover the important details. Being a social researcher and methodologist, working on surveys, qualitative studies, and usability research, has given me the opportunity to build that fascination into a career.

With over 15 years of experience leading public opinion research, I’m an accomplished survey methodologist and usability researcher with expertise in qualitative and mixed method approaches, program and project management, and team leadership. I thrive working across interdisciplinary teams and directing complex studies that provide data-driven insights for policymakers and external stakeholders, and have had the opportunity to lead collaborations between governmental and non-governmental agencies including the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, the World Bank, the World Health Organization, and UNICEF.

Making sure that people’s stories are heard is what drives me. I want to ensure that users can navigate interfaces and sites easily, I want to validate that survey respondents understand the questions they’re being asked—my goal is to bring the voice of everyday folks into policy and research decisions.

Skills

Here are a few things I bring to the table...

Survey Design

With 15+ years designing questionnaires and 10+ years developing custom sampling and weighting plans for household, business, and user surveys, I really enjoy collaborating with stakeholders and data users to find the optimal survey design approaches to efficiently collect data. The focus of my professional career has been on the front-end of data collection—making sure that surveys and studies are designed in a way to get highest-possible quality data.

Qualitative Research

I am an expert qualitative interviewer, with nearly 2 decades of experience conducting and leading qualitative research, including ethnographic interviews, cognitive interviews, and focus groups. I’ve had the opportunity to conduct qualitative interviews around the United States and the world—from India and France to Morocco and Zambia—and relish the analytic process of trying to distill peoples’ stories into actionable data.

Statistical Analysis

Skilled conducting descriptive and inferential statistical studies, including ANOVA, ANCOVA, and chi-square tests. My preferred software is R (and the tidyverse), though I have hands-on practice using Python, SPSS, and SAS as well. I’ve recently been expanding my knowledge of machine learning approaches as well and have experience building and deploying ML pipelines using scikit-learn.

User Research

I have extensive experience conducting face-to-face and virtual user experience and usability studies, including evaluations of marketing materials, user interfaces, and healthcare technologies. I’m skilled with both qualitative and quantitative analyses, especially focusing on user demographics and characteristics.

Communication and Stakeholder Management

As a methodologist and social scientist, I often find myself acting as a translator of sorts between a project’s various stakeholders—the funders/product managers, technical staff such as statisticians, data collection contractors, and senior leadership. It’s a role I thrive in, as it allows me to pull all my various interests and expertise together while solving the puzzle of getting everyone involved in a project the information and outcomes they need.

People Leadership

I have been supervising junior staff for over five years, and managing mangers for one. I’m responsible for training junior staff in the theory and practice of qualitative and statistical analysis and for developing a program to introduce the foundations of mixed method research to new staff. I’ve found my role as a mentor—helping colleagues grow into world-class researchers and develop their own approaches to project management—to be incredibly fulfilling.
